Strategy Description — Sourish Srivastava, 2025MT61515
====================================

1. Core Idea
------------
The strategy aims to exploit persistent cross-sectional relationships between technical indicators and next-day stock returns. 
Stocks are ranked daily using a weighted combination of momentum, mean-reversion, volume, and volatility-related factors. 
The weights are proportional to each factor's historical Information Coefficient (IC). 
Positions are then scaled by estimated risk to construct a dollar-neutral long-short portfolio.

2. Signal Construction
-----------------------
The strategy combines the following technical indicators:
Trend: trend_1_3, trend_5_20, trend_20_60
Momentum: relative_strength_index, stochastic_oscillator, williams_r, ultimate_oscillator, commodity_channel_index, chande_momentum_oscillator, trix, macd
Volume: volume, on_balance_volume, accumulation_distribution_index, chaikin_money_flow, ease_of_movement
Volatility: average_true_range (used as a predictive feature), with volatility_20 and volatility_60 used for risk adjustment.

SIGNAL COMBINATION: 
Each factor is transformed into a cross-sectional percentile rank to reduce sensitivity to outliers and differences in scale. 
The ranked factors are combined using fixed weights proportional to their historical cross-sectional Information Coefficients (ICs), 
with the sign determined by the historical predictive direction of each factor.

FEATURE SMOOTHING:
Instead of using only the most recent observation, each feature is smoothed using a 10-day Exponentially Weighted Moving Average (EWMA) computed over the recent history. 
This reduces day-to-day noise while allowing the signals to adapt gradually to changing market conditions. 
During experimentation, EWMA smoothing significantly reduced portfolio turnover while slightly improving risk-adjusted performance.

3. Portfolio Construction
---------------------------
Stocks are assigned a continuous alpha score obtained from the weighted combination of ranked factors.
To control risk, the alpha score is divided by the square root of the average of the 20-day and 60-day realized volatility estimates, reducing exposure to more volatile stocks.
The resulting scores are centered to ensure zero net exposure before portfolio construction.
Positive and negative scores are separated into long and short portfolios. 
Each side is independently normalized to 50% gross exposure, producing a dollar-neutral portfolio with total gross exposure equal to one. 
Individual positions are capped at ± 10% to satisfy the constraints, after which both long and short books are renormalized.

4. Risk Controls
------------------
a- Dollar-neutral portfolio construction.
b- Gross exposure fixed at 100%.
c- Individual position limit of ±10%.
d- Volatility-adjusted position sizing using 20-day and 60-day realized volatility.
e- EWMA smoothing of features to reduce unnecessary portfolio turnover and improve signal stability.

5. Known Limitations / Things You'd Try Next
-----------------------------------------------
Several adaptive weighting approaches were investigated, including 
a- rolling Information Coefficients, 
b- rolling ICs with fixed global factor directions 
c- Information Ratio-based weighting 
While these methods appeared promising initially, testing showed that they did not outperform the simpler global IC weighting on the training data.
Future work would focus on improving the alpha itself rather than dynamically adjusting factor weights. 
Possibilities include economically motivated factor interaction terms, confidence-based position sizing, and hyperparameter tuning of smoothing windows and risk-adjustment parameters.
